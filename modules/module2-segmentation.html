Module 2 · Revenue Tracking Segmentation

Every booking tells a story — if you track it correctly

Tracking segmentation is how Dusit records who books, through which channel, and where they come from. Get it right and every report, every forecast, and every rate decision is built on accurate data. Get it wrong and the whole system breaks down silently.

Market Segment
Who is booking and why — the rate type and guest category that drives all pricing logic.
Source Code
The specific channel the booking came through — website, GDS, OTA, phone, walk-in.
Origin Code
Was there an intermediary? Direct booking or through a third party?
Country of Residence
Where the guest lives — mandatory worldwide using the ISO standard.
Why it matters: These four fields feed directly into Dusit's DDP reports, RevPAR analysis, and forecasting. A wrong market code in the PMS produces wrong data everywhere — instantly.
What is tracking segmentation?

Every booking has a story.
Tracking makes sure
we record it correctly.

When a reservation comes in, two things happen — someone books a room, and someone stays in it. These are often different people. A travel agent books for a corporate guest. A parent books for their family. An OTA books on behalf of a tourist.

Tracking segmentation records both sides of that story — who booked and who stayed — so the hotel can analyse demand, set the right rates, and report accurately.

Why it matters
A wrong market code in the PMS produces wrong data in every report, every forecast, and every rate decision — immediately and silently. There is no error message. The system just stores what you enter.

Tracking segmentation captures two groups of data fields — one about the booking, one about the guest.

FIT
Free Individual Traveller

Retail, Corporate & Wholesale

FIT covers all individual travellers — from walk-in tourists paying BAR to negotiated corporate accounts and contracted wholesale rates. The sub-segment determines which rate logic applies and which discount band is used.

Critical distinction
DLOYAL (seq 58) is exclusively for Dusit Gold loyalty members. Do not use it for any other purpose — including Best Rate Guarantee.

ECOMM (seq 45) must be used for all OTA bookings. No exceptions.
Retail Non-Qualified · Retail Qualified · Negotiated Corporate · Wholesale
DBRRTBest Flexible RateInternal
Highest publicly available rate. Room only (BARRO) or room with breakfast (BARRB). All dynamic rates are indexed from this rate. This is the BAR equivalent in the PMS.
ADVPCAdvance Purchase / Saverseq 50
Discounted public rates with less flexibility — prepaid, minimum length of stay. Includes Saver, Early Bird, and Stay Longer offers for room only and room with breakfast.
HTPCKPackageseq 30

ECOMME-Commerce / OTAseq 45

GRP
Group Segments

Business Groups & Leisure Groups

Group bookings have their own segment codes separate from FIT. The key rule: any free-of-charge rooms that are part of a group contract carry the same market code as the core group business. Do not reclassify complimentary rooms within a group booking.

Remember
The market code is set at the time of booking and does not change — even for groups. If the group is corporate, every room in that block is GCORP, including complimentary rooms.

OTH
Other Segments

Crew, Contracted & Internal

The Other category covers everything that does not fit FIT or Group — airline crew, long-stay contracts, complimentary rooms, and house use. These codes exist to keep operational rooms separate from revenue-generating rooms in all reporting.

Finance note
The ACT segment is Finance Department use only. It must always be used with a PM room and the ACTINT rate code. No other department should use this code under any circumstances.

Origin Codes

Track the origin of business

Origin codes identify whether a reservation was made directly by the guest or through an intermediary. There are only two origin codes and both are set automatically via SynXis. The key question is simple: is there an intermediary between the guest and the hotel?

DIR
Direct
No intermediary
The reservation was made directly by the guest who will check in and check out. No third party is involved in the booking process.
Guest books dusit.com Guest calls hotel Walk-in
IND
Indirect
Intermediary present
The reservation was made through an intermediary — a third party sits between the guest and the hotel in the booking chain.
OTA Travel Agency TMC GDS Booker
Both codes are set automatically by SynXis. You do not need to enter origin codes manually — but you should understand what they mean when you see them in a report.
COR
Country of Residence

Where does the guest live?

Country of residence is now mandatory worldwide for all Dusit properties. It uses the ISO standard country code system — the same codes used by airlines, governments, and international systems. Always ask yourself: where does this guest live? Not where are they from.

Common mistakes to avoid
SpainSP ES ✓
FranceF FR ✓
ThailandTH ✓
United KingdomGB ✓
Why residence — not nationality

Country of residence tells us where the guest actually lives and travels from — not their passport. This is what matters for market analysis, source market reporting, and demand forecasting.

Scenario
A French national living and working in Singapore books a room. Their passport is French — but their country of residence is SG (Singapore), not FR. The hotel's source market report should show Singapore, because that is where the demand is coming from.
How to fill it in
Ask the guest at time of booking: "What country are you currently based in?" Enter the two-letter ISO code in the Country field of the reservation. If unsure of the code, refer to the ISO 3166-1 alpha-2 reference list.
